# Content Guidelines

## General (all subjects)

- Language targets a 4–6 year old reading/listening level (Flesch-Kincaid Grade ~1)
- No violence, fear-inducing content, or dark themes without specific review
- Diverse representation in all visual content — not just culture subject
- Activities must be completable in 3–7 minutes

## Black History & Culture — specific guidelines

### What this subject IS
- Celebrating Black excellence, creativity, and joy
- Age-appropriate stories of real people and historical moments
- Everyday Black cultural traditions, foods, music, art
- Black identity as something to be proud of, not explained or defended

### What this subject IS NOT
- A trauma curriculum — suffering and oppression are not the frame
- A "diversity lesson" for non-Black audiences — this is FOR Black children
- Exhaustive historical coverage — curation over comprehensiveness

### Content review checklist

Before any culture content goes live:
- [ ] Is the historical/factual content accurate?
- [ ] Is the framing centered on humanity and resilience, not victimhood?
- [ ] Would a 4–6 year old understand and enjoy this without explanation from a parent?
- [ ] Does it reflect the diversity within Black communities (diasporic, regional, etc.)?
- [ ] Has it been reviewed by a Black educator or cultural consultant?

### Topics pre-approved for first release
- Counting (Kwanzaa candles)
- Music (jazz, blues, go-go, hip-hop origins)
- Inventors and scientists (Mae Jemison, George Washington Carver, Garrett Morgan)
- Civil rights figures (Rosa Parks, MLK — age-appropriate framing on the "brave and changed things" angle)
- Foods and celebrations
- Hair and beauty — natural hair content specifically

### Topics requiring additional review before inclusion
- Slavery — only with careful, child-centered framing emphasizing humanity and resistance
- Current events / systemic racism — too complex for age range; defer to parent resources
