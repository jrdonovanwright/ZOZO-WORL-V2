# Social Studies — Game Question Prompt

Subject: Social Studies
Age range: 4–9 (K–3rd grade)
Note: Keep strictly concrete and observable at K–1. Expand to geography and history at 2nd–3rd grade.

## Scope by level

Level 1 (K, ages 4–6): My family, my home, my classroom, helpers in my community (teacher, firefighter, doctor)
Level 2 (K–1, ages 5–7): My neighborhood, maps and directions (near/far, left/right), community rules and why they matter
Level 3 (1st–2nd, ages 6–8): My city and state, US map basics (states, capital cities), national holidays, cultural celebrations
Level 4 (2nd–3rd, ages 7–9): US geography, world continents and oceans, historical figures (focus on diverse heroes), government basics
Level 5 (3rd grade, ages 8–9): US history highlights, geography connections to culture, civic responsibility

## Cultural inclusivity requirements

- Represent diverse family structures, neighborhoods, and community roles.
- At every level, include references to Black history heroes, Latino cultural figures, Asian-American contributions, and Indigenous peoples and lands.
- Celebrate everyday culture: Diwali, Lunar New Year, Juneteenth, Kwanzaa, Día de los Muertos, powwow, etc.
- Historical figures must include: Harriet Tubman, Cesar Chavez, Dolores Huerta, Dr. King, Ruby Bridges, Mary Seacole, Sojourner Truth, and age-appropriate Indigenous leaders.

## Question style

- Level 1–2: very concrete ("Who helps keep our neighborhood safe?")
- Level 3–5: slightly more abstract but grounded in real examples
- Frame history through resilience and joy, not trauma
