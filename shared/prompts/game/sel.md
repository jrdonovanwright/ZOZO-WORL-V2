# Social-Emotional Learning (SEL) — Game Question Prompt

Subject: Social-Emotional Learning
Age range: 4–9 (K–3rd grade)
Note: SEL is UNSCORED (scored: false). There are no wrong answers — only reflection and validation.

## Scope by level

Level 1: Naming basic emotions (happy, sad, mad, scared, surprised), identifying feelings in faces
Level 2: Why we feel certain ways, what helps when we feel sad or mad, kindness actions
Level 3: Empathy — how might someone else feel? Conflict resolution, taking turns, saying sorry
Level 4: Self-regulation strategies (deep breath, count to 10, take a walk), understanding triggers
Level 5: Complex emotions (proud, frustrated, embarrassed, grateful), growth mindset, helping others

## SEL question style (CRITICAL)

- These are NOT tests. The child CANNOT get a wrong answer.
- Present a feeling or scenario, child taps what THEY would do or how THEY feel.
- Zoey validates every response warmly and adds a reflection.
- zoey_correct and zoey_wrong should BOTH be warm and non-judgmental:
  - zoey_correct (for the "expected" response): "That's one way to feel! Lots of kids feel that way too."
  - zoey_wrong: same warmth — "That makes sense too! Every feeling is okay to have."
- Scenarios should reflect diverse family and cultural contexts.
- Focus on feelings as information, not problems to fix.

## Example questions

- "Zoey's friend took her crayon without asking. How do you think Zoey feels?" [choices: Mad 😤, Happy 😊, Excited 🤩]
- "When something scary happens, what helps you feel better?" [choices: Talk to someone 🤗, Take deep breaths 😮‍💨, Hug something soft 🧸]
- "Your friend is crying because they lost their toy. What could you do?" [choices: Give them a hug, Tell a funny joke, Find the toy together]
