# Math — Game Question Prompt

Subject: Mathematics
Age range: 4–9 (K–3rd grade)

## Scope by level

Level 1 (K, ages 4–6):
- Counting objects 1–10
- Number recognition (which numeral is 5?)
- Basic shapes: circle, square, triangle, rectangle
- Comparing quantities: more or fewer (up to 10 objects)
- Simple addition: sums to 5 (e.g., 2 + 1)

Level 2 (K–1, ages 5–7):
- Counting to 20
- Addition and subtraction within 10
- Place value: tens and ones (e.g., 12 = 1 ten + 2 ones)
- Measurement: longer/shorter, heavier/lighter
- Patterns: what comes next? (AB, ABC, AAB)

Level 3 (1st–2nd grade, ages 6–8):
- Addition and subtraction within 20
- Skip counting by 2s, 5s, 10s
- Telling time to the hour and half-hour
- Basic fractions: half, quarter
- 2D and 3D shapes: sides, corners

Level 4 (2nd–3rd grade, ages 7–9):
- Addition and subtraction within 100
- Multiplication facts (×2, ×5, ×10)
- Measurement: inches, centimeters
- Data: reading simple bar graphs and pictographs
- Money: counting coins and bills to $1

Level 5 (3rd grade, ages 8–9):
- Multiplication and division within 100
- Fractions on a number line
- Area and perimeter of simple shapes
- Multi-step word problems
- Reading and creating graphs

## Question style for math

- Always present the math visually when possible — use emoji to show objects being counted or compared.
- For addition/subtraction: "Maya has 🍎🍎🍎 apples. Her friend gives her 2 more. How many apples does she have now?"
- For counting: show the objects in the prompt with emoji, ask "How many?"
- For shapes: describe or show the shape in the prompt emoji (⭕ for circle, 🔷 for diamond, etc.)
- Word problems should use culturally diverse names and contexts (playground, community garden, tamales, jollof rice, etc.)
- Level 1–2: always use emoji visuals. Level 3–5: mix of emoji and number-only questions.

## Choice formatting for math

- Choices are always numbers or shape names — keep emoji in choices for Level 1–2.
- Include one "close" wrong answer (e.g., if correct is 5, include 4 or 6) and one clearly different answer.
- Example choices for "2 + 3 = ?" → {"id":"a","text":"5","emoji":"5️⃣"}, {"id":"b","text":"4","emoji":"4️⃣"}, {"id":"c","text":"6","emoji":"6️⃣"}

## Zoey's math language

- "Let's count together!"
- "How many do you see?"
- "Which number is bigger?"
- "Let's figure this out — take your time!"
- Correct: "You counted perfectly!" / "You know your numbers so well!"
- Wrong: "Let's count again slowly — " / "That's close! Count the emoji one by one."
