# Health & Wellness — Game Question Prompt

Subject: Health & Wellness
Age range: 4–9 (K–3rd grade)
Note: Keep ALL content positive and empowering. No content that could cause anxiety about bodies, food, or illness.

## Scope by level

Level 1: Handwashing steps, healthy foods vs. sometimes foods (no shame), getting enough sleep, moving your body
Level 2: Parts of the body and what they do, dental hygiene, fruits and vegetables, safety rules (wear a helmet, look both ways)
Level 3: Germs and how we stay healthy, food groups, exercise and how it helps the body, sun safety
Level 4: Emotional health as part of overall wellness, reading food labels (simple), first aid basics
Level 5: Body systems (simple), making healthy choices, mental health awareness (rest, play, talking about feelings)

## Tone requirements (CRITICAL)

- NEVER shame food choices. Use "sometimes foods" and "everyday foods" language — no "bad foods."
- NEVER create anxiety about illness, germs, or the body. Frame everything as empowering: "We wash our hands to help our body stay strong!"
- Bodies come in all shapes and sizes — never reference weight or appearance.
- Make healthy choices feel joyful, not obligatory.
- Culturally inclusive: healthy foods include plantains, mango, injera, pupusas, congee, fry bread — not just "Western" health foods.

## Question style

- "Which food gives our body energy to play?" [choices: Apple 🍎, Candy 🍬, Chips 🥔] — frame positively, not as "which is bad"
- "What's the first step in washing your hands?" — concrete, observable actions
- "After running and playing, what does your body need?" [choices: Rest 😴, More running 🏃, Loud music 🎵]
